A complete guide to managing your patient media files

1. General presentation

Kitview is a digital media library designed for orthodontic practices. It can be used to manage all types of multimedia files associated with patient records:

  • Photos: portraits, intra-oral photos, front/profile photos
  • X-rays: panoramic, teleradiography
  • 3D scans: digital models
  • Documents: PDFs, letters, minutes
  • Videos and audio

Kitview's main interface with patient list and thumbnails

Council Kitview integrates with your practice management software to automatically synchronise your patient list.

2. Patient management

Create a new patient

To create a new patient record in Kitview :

  1. Click on the 'Patient' tab
  2. A window will open: fill in the details (Surname, First name, Date of birth, Sex)
  3. Click on "Validate

New patient creation window

Delete a patient record

️ Caution Deleting a patient record is permanent and also deletes all associated photos and documents. This action requires Administration mode.
  1. Click on 'Settings
  2. Click on "Administration" then "General".
  3. Tick "Switch to administration mode
  4. Select the patient to be deleted
  5. Click on "Patient" → "Delete the selected patient".

Passport photo

To set or change a patient's passport photo :

  1. Select the patient from the list
  2. Click on the photo you wish to use as your ID photo
  3. Right-click on the photo
  4. Select "Patient" → "Passport photo".

Pop-up menu to define the passport photo

3. Customising the interface

Tabs and presentation style

Kitview offers different interface display modes that you can customise to suit your preferences.

Adding/deleting tabs

  1. Click on 'Settings
  2. Select "Display" → "Appearance".
  3. Tick or untick the desired tabs

Office colours

To customise the interface colours :

  1. Click on 'Offices
  2. Select "Desktop colours
  3. Choose the colours you want for each element
  4. Click on "OK" to confirm

Thumbnail size

You can adjust the size of the thumbnails displayed:

  1. Click on 'Settings
  2. Select "Display" → "Results".
  3. Adjust the "Thumbnail size" value

Customise the photo selection colour

  1. Click on 'Settings
  2. Select "Display" → "Results".
  3. Change the "Selection colour
Council Remember to save your desktop once you have made the changes: Bureaux → Enregistrer le bureau courant

4. Document import/export

Import photos

There are several ways of importing documents into a patient file:

Method 1: Drag and drop from Windows

  1. Select the patient concerned
  2. Open Windows Explorer with your photos
  3. Select the photos to import
  4. Drag and drop photos into Kitview's thumbnail area

Method 2: Import from Outlook

You can import email attachments directly:

  1. Open the email in Outlook
  2. Select the attachment(s)
  3. Drag and drop into Kitview

Exporting photos

To export photos to Windows or a USB memory stick :

  1. Select the photo(s) to be exported
  2. Right-click
  3. Select "Export"→"Quick export".
  4. Choose the destination folder

Advanced drag & drop

Drag and drop works both ways:

  • To Kitview: to import files
  • From Kitview: to export to Windows, email, etc.

Configuring the attachment name format

To customise file names when exporting :

  1. Click on 'Settings
  2. Select "Mail
  3. Configure the desired format using the available variables (%n = last name, %p = first name, etc.).

5. Photo sessions

Session protocol

The protocol guides the taking of photos in a predefined order using templates.

  1. Select the patient
  2. Click on "Session
  3. Click on "Start session
  4. Follow the templates shown to take the photos in the correct order

Session mode interface with templates

WiFi acquisition

Kitview lets you receive photos directly from a WiFi camera:

  1. Configure your camera in WiFi mode
  2. In Kitview, click on "Acquisition
  3. Select "WiFi
  4. Photos are received automatically
️ Information The camera and computer must be connected to the same WiFi network.

6. Templates and keywords

Templates can be used to automatically classify your photos using associated keywords. There are 3 steps to creating a custom template:

Step 1: Create the keyword in the dictionary

  1. Click on 'Settings
  2. Select "Dictionary" → "Thesaurus for your field".
  3. Click on "Expand all" to see all families
  4. Position yourself in the right family
  5. Click on "Create a new label

Thesaurus management interface

Step 2: Create the template

  1. Click on 'Settings
  2. Select "Templates" → "Toolbox".
  3. Right-click on a family → "Add" to create a new family
  4. Click on "Create a new template".
  5. Click on "Load an image" to add an image to the template
  6. Fill in the "Description" field
  7. Double-click on the family to associate the keyword with it.
  8. Click on "Validate

Template creation window

Step 3: Create the filter (collection)

  1. Right-click on "All" in the tree structure
  2. Select "Add a collection to the root".
  3. Give the collection a name (identical to the keyword)
  4. Click on "Add a condition
  5. Select the keyword you created earlier
  6. Click on "Validate
Council Once you have created your templates, go to the "Template" tab and click on the "Refresh" button to see your new creations appear.

Modify the image of an existing template

  1. Click on "Settings"→"Templates"→"Toolbox".
  2. Select the template to be modified
  3. Click on "Modify the selected template".
  4. Click on "Upload an image
  5. Select the new image
  6. Click on "Validate

7. Viewers

Presentation of viewers

Viewers are customisable display areas for viewing photos in large format. You can have as many as you like.

Kitview offers several display modes:

  • 1 View: a single large photo
  • 2 Views: two photos side by side for comparison
  • 4 Views: four photos for an overview
  • Templates: display according to templates
  • Portfolio: gallery view

Mode 1 View

2-view mode

4 views mode

Select the photos to display

  1. Position yourself in the "1 View", "2 Views" or "4 Views" tab.
  2. Click or drag and drop the photo you want
Drag and drop reminder Select the photo, hold down the left click and release at the right point.

Customise viewers

Set viewers to the same size

  1. In 4 views mode, click on the "Reorder sister windows" icon in the top right-hand corner.
  2. To adjust manually: position yourself on the dividing line (a double arrow appears)
  3. Hold down the left click and adjust

Customise the viewer toolbar

  1. Right-click in the viewer area
  2. Click on "Customise menus".
  3. Select the toolbars you require
  4. To apply to all viewers: right-click → "Apply this menu to other viewers".

Modify the information overlay

To change the information displayed on photos (date, name, etc.) :

  1. Click on 'Settings
  2. Select "Display" → "Miscellaneous".
  3. Double-click on 'Viewers
  4. Change the size, colour and information displayed
  5. Use the "?" to see the codes available (%c = date, %n = name, etc.).

Add viewers

  1. Click on "Settings" → "Administration" → "General".
  2. Tick Expert Mode
  3. Click on "Desktops" → "Reorganise desktop".
  4. Icons appear on the interface
  5. In the "Viewer" tab, click on "Add a viewer".
  6. A viewer window opens: move it to the required position using the arrow keys
  7. To rename the tab: right-click → "Rename".
️ Important Don't forget to save your desktop after making the changes: Bureaux → Enregistrer le bureau courant

8. Editing photos

Copy and paste a photo

To duplicate a photo in the patient file :

Method 1: Context menu

  1. Right-click on the photo to be duplicated
  2. Click on 'Edit
  3. Click on 'Copy
  4. Click on 'Paste

Method 2: Edit menu

  1. Select photo
  2. Click on the "Edit" menu at the top
  3. Click on "Copy" then "Paste".

Changing the brightness

  1. Position yourself on the "1 View" tab
  2. Select the photo to be edited
  3. Click on thepalette icon in the viewer toolbar
  4. Adjust brightness and contrast with the sliders
  5. The photo on the left shows the result, the one on the right the original.
  6. Click on 'OK

Brightness/contrast adjustment interface

Return to original photo

If you have modified a photo and wish to return to the original :

  1. Right-click on the edited photo
  2. Select 'Image
  3. Click on "Revert to original".
  4. Refresh if necessary
Council If you want to make changes to a photo, duplicate it first to preserve the original.

Editing with Paint

For more advanced retouching with an external editor :

  1. Right-click on the photo
  2. Select "Explorer Menu
  3. Click on "Open with"→"Paint".
  4. Make your changes
  5. Save with the floppy disk so that the changes appear in Kitview

Cropping a photo

To select and keep only an area of the photo :

  1. Select photo
  2. Click on the "1 View" tab
  3. In the toolbar, use the "Rectangle selection" tool
  4. Draw a rectangle around the area to be preserved
  5. Click on the 'Trimmer' tool to crop the image
  6. Click on the diskette to save

Viewer bar tools

  • Rotate right/left: rotate the photo
  • Horizontal/vertical symmetry: mirror effect
  • Adjusting colour: brightness, contrast
  • Add text: annotations on the photo

Full viewer toolbar

9. Fixed presentations

Fixed presentations can be used to automatically generate comparative views based on keywords associated with the photos. They are ideal for showing the progress of a treatment to patients or colleagues.

️ Prerequisites For presentations to work properly, your photos need to have the right keywords associated with them.

Access to presentations

  1. Select a patient
  2. Click on the 'Presentation' tab
  3. Choose from the presentations available at the bottom of the screen

Fixed presentation interface

Types of presentation available

Complete sequence

Overview with portrait, intra-oral and panoramic photos.

Start End ¾

Before-and-after comparison in three-quarter view.

Complete sequence 2

Full view including profile and teleradiography.

Moulds

Presentation of photos of dental casts.

Start In progress End

Three-step comparison to monitor treatment progress.

Compare

Customisable side-by-side comparison tool.

10. Docking (customising the desktop)

Each desktop is fully customisable. You can add, remove or move items as required.

Activate customisation mode

  1. Click on 'Settings
  2. Select "Administration" → "General".
  3. Tick Expert Mode
  4. Click on 'Offices
  5. Select "Reorganise desktop

Actions available

  • Enlarge/shrink an area: position yourself between two areas (an arrow will appear), then drag
  • Delete a zone: click on the cross in the zone
  • Move a zone: hold the click on the zone title and move using the directional arrows.

Example of a personalised desk

️ Important Always remember to save your desktop after making changes: Bureaux → Enregistrer le bureau courant

11. Create a custom fixed presentation

You can create your own fixed presentations with the thumbnails and keywords of your choice.

Creation stages

  1. Click on the 'Settings' tab
  2. Click on 'Administration
  3. Click on "General
  4. Tick "Expert mode
  5. Click on the 'Presentation' tab
  6. A window opens: click on "Free presentations".
  7. Click on "Create a free presentation".
  8. Name your presentation

Add thumbnails to the presentation

  1. Click on the small+.
  2. A window opens: click on "Thumbnail with attributes".
  3. Hold down the left mouse button and draw a square or rectangle, then double-click in the thumbnail you have created
  4. A window opens: search for the keyword you want, then double-click on it
  5. The keyword is displayed in the table on the right
  6. Click on "Validate" - Repeat for each thumbnail

Resize and align thumbnails

For a multiple selection, holdCtrandclick on each thumbnail, then use the alignment tools :

  • Same width: standardises width
  • Aligned top/bottom: aligns vertically
  • Left/right aligned: aligns horizontally

Click on the diskette to save your presentation.

13. Comparing photos

Kitview offers a number of tools for comparing photos and showing the evolution of a treatment.

Comparing two photos (2 views mode)

  1. Click on the "2 Views" tab
  2. Select the two photos to be compared
  3. To lock a photo, click on the small padlock
  4. The locked photo remains fixed while the other can scroll

Column or line comparator

The comparator lets you compare several selected photos:

  1. Select the photos to compare
  2. Click on the icon of the desired comparator:Column comparator(manual or automatic)Online comparator(manual or automatic)

Manual mode

Use the arrows to scroll through the photos. The reference photo (left or top) remains frozen.

Automatic mode

Photos are automatically scrolled by shooting date.

Council To exit the comparator, press Esc . Échap

Protocol session comparator

If you follow a precise protocol when taking photos, this feature allows you to compare sessions over time.

  1. Click on the 'Presentation' tab
  2. Click on the 'Comparator' tab
  3. Select the photos to be shown (e.g. the 3 intra-oral photos)
  4. Choose mode: Horizontal/Vertical, Manual/Automatic

The photos of the first appointment remain static (at the top or on the left) and those of subsequent appointments scroll by date.

14. Mail templates

Kitview allows you to use mail templates with merge fields that automatically include patient information (surname, first name, address, photos, etc.).

️ Prerequisites Photos must be clearly identified (keywords) for the merge fields to work properly.

Use an existing model

  1. Select the patient
  2. Click on the 'Mail' tab
  3. Select the desired model from the list
  4. The letter opens with the patient's details pre-filled in

See the merge fields

Switch to "Create" mode to view and modify the merge fields (e.g. <First name>, <Last name>, <Today's date>...).

Create a new letter template

  1. Click on the 'Mail' tab
  2. Select an existing model as a base
  3. Click on "Creation
  4. Click on 'New' for a blank document
  5. In "Page Properties", tick "Landscape" if necessary

Adding items

  • Add text: Click on "Add" → "Add text", draw a zone, double-click to enter
  • Add an image (merge field): Click on "Add" → "Image field", draw a zone, double-click and select the desired field

Save the model

  1. Click on "Preview" to check the result
  2. Click on "Save
  3. Select the 'Letters' folder
  4. Name your file and click on "Save".

15. Portfolio and PowerPoint presentation

The Portfolio can be used to group together photos of several patients and automatically generate a PowerPoint presentation.

Example: All intra-oral scans of all patients

  1. Click on the 'All' tab in the tree structure
  2. Select the desired category (e.g. "Intra-oral")
  3. Click on "Filter population by collection".
  4. Click on "Select all patients
  5. Click on the 'Portfolio' tab
  6. All the corresponding photos are displayed
  7. Select the photos you want to include and drag and drop them into the area on the right
  8. Click on the PowerPoint icon and choose the number of images per slide

Result in PowerPoint

The presentation opens automatically in PowerPoint. You can then add text, change the page layout, etc.

️ Important Remember to save your PowerPoint presentation after making any changes.

16. Population filter

Population filters allow you to search by type of object or photo for one or more patients. Filters are organised by category.

Example: Display all panoramic X-rays

  1. Click on the 'All' tab
  2. Click on 'Radio' then 'Panoramic' (or another category)
  3. Click on "Filter population by collection".
  4. Click on "Select all patients
  5. Result: all your patients' panoramic X-rays are displayed
  6. You can go to each patient file to find the corresponding radio
Tip Remember to return to your complete database after a search: go back to the population filters, click on the "All" tab and then "Refresh".

17. Find photos by date

Population filters can also be used to search for photos taken on a specific date or within a date range.

Search for a specific date

Example:Search for all photos taken on 24/11/2017

  1. Click on "What
  2. Click on "Add a condition to the root".
  3. A window opens - Enter the word "Creation" - It appears in the list
  4. Double-click on the word "Creation" to display it in the table on the right
  5. Click on "Validate
  6. Change the condition to 'Equal to' and then change the date
  7. All patients with photos taken on this date are displayed
  8. All photos taken on this date are displayed

To view photos of each patient, click on each patient file.

Reminder Don't forget to return to your complete database after the search: "All" tab, then "Refresh".

Search within a date range

Example:Search for all photos taken between 13/09/2017 and 13/09/2018

  1. Click on "What
  2. Click on "Add a condition to the root".
  3. Write "Creation" and double-click to add it
  4. Click on "Validate
  5. Repeat the operation to add a second condition
  6. For the first condition, select 'Prior to' and enter the end date.
  7. For the second condition, select 'After' and enter the start date.
  8. All patients with photos in this range are displayed
️ Note The two conditions are combined with a logical AND: the photos displayed are both before the end date AND after the start date.

18. Forms

Forms are used to store all the information relating to a patient, to provide rapid access to information, and to present and use the data entered.

Fill in a form

  1. Click on the 'Form' tab
  2. Select the form you need
  3. Complete the information
  4. Save the form by clicking on the label
  5. The summary of the form is generated. To view it in full, click on the small folder

Search for similar cases

Example:Search forms for patients with gingivitis

  1. Click on a patient file
  2. Click on the 'Form' tab
  3. Open the summary of forms by clicking on the small folder
  4. Click on the "Search mode" magnifying glass
  5. Click on the criteria you want to find (e.g. "gingivitis")
  6. Click on the "Search mode" magnifying glass again, then on "Search".
  7. The corresponding patient files are displayed
Tip To view each form, go to the patient file and click on the 'Forms' tab.
